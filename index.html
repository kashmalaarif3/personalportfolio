Analysis of Community Violent Crime Rates Using Socioeconomic Features (Data Science)

Made with
The US Census Bureau has released a comprehensive dataset, sourced from the UCI database, combining socio-economic data from the 1990 US Census, law enforcement data from the 1990 US LEMAS survey, and crime data from the 1995 FBI UCR, providing detailed insights into the characteristics of communities within the United States. With 1,994 instances each representing a community and 127 features, this dataset presents an invaluable opportunity to delve into the socio-economic landscape and crime dynamics across different communities.I aim to leverage this dataset to develop predictive models for crime rates within these communities. By analyzing the interplay between socioeconomic factors and crime data, I aim to answer three specific questions regarding the dataset.

McDiver Game (Software Engineering)

Made with
This game uses techniques such as graph algorithms and concurrency. I also optimized my graph searches using various data structures like heaps and queues. In this game our protagonist, McDiver, navigates the sewer network, represented as a graph. McDiver is tasked with finding a special power ring that has been hidden in the sewer. The job is to navigate the maze to find the ring. However, this does not have to be a completely blind search. Fortunately there is a detector that can measure the distance to the ring, so the McDiver can tell when they are getting closer. When McDiver finds the ring, it turns out that it is booby-trapped. McDiver is dropped into a lower level of tunnels where there is additional treasure sprinkled all through the maze. Fortunately, McDiver immediately finds the map to this lower level and wants to collect some treasure on the way out. But toxic fumes in the sewer are starting to take a toll. So the goal is to head to the exit in the prescribed number of steps while still picking up as much treasure as possible along the way.
